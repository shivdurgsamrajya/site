Pratapgad Fort Maharashtra

  • Travel, Lifestyle, Trekking, Explore
  • 00 Comments
  • Wikipedia

Pratapgad is a mountain fort located in Satara district, in the Western Indian state of Maharashtra. The fort is situated 24 kilometres from the Mahabaleshwar hill station. The fort is now a popular tourist destination.

The fort's historical significance is due to the Battle of Pratapgad, which took place here on 10 November 1659, between Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and Bijapur Sultanate general Afzal Khan. Chatrapati Shivaji's killing of Afzal Khan was followed by decisive Maratha victory over the Bijapur army.

HISTORY OF THE ATTRACTION

The Battle of Pratapgad between Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan, a general of Adil Shahi dynasty, was fought below the ramparts of this fort on 10 November 1659. This was the first major test of the fledgling kingdom's army, and set the stage of the establishment of the Maratha empire.

The Maratha ruler Shivaji assigned Moropant Trimbak Pingle, his prime minister, to undertake the construction of this fort in order to defend the banks of the Nira and the Koyna rivers, and to defend the Par pass. It was completed in 1656.

How to Reach:

By Air

The nearest major airport to Pratapgad is Pune Airport (PNQ), which is approximately 120 kilometers away. You can book a flight to Pune from major Indian cities like Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Chennai. Once you arrive at Pune Airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Pratapgad. The journey from Pune to Pratapgad by road takes about 3-4 hours.

By Train

The nearest railway station to Pratapgad is Satara Railway Station (SUR), which is approximately 24 kilometers away from the fort. You can reach Satara by train from major cities like Mumbai, Pune, and Bangalore. From Satara Railway Station, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Pratapgad. The journey by road from Satara to Pratapgad takes around 1 hour.

By Road

Pratapgad is well-connected by road, and you can reach the fort by car or bus. Here are some common routes:

From Mumbai: Take NH48 (Mumbai-Pune Expressway) to Pune and then continue on NH48 to reach Satara. From Satara, follow the road signs to Pratapgad.

From Pune: Take NH48 (Mumbai-Pune Expressway) towards Satara. Once you reach Satara, follow the directions to Pratapgad.

From Bangalore: Head towards Pune on NH48 or NH4, and then follow the directions mentioned above.

From Satara: If you're already in Satara, you can drive directly to Pratapgad by following the local roads and road signs.

Geography

Pratapgad fort is located at 15 km (10 miles) from Poladpur and 23 km (15 miles) west of Mahabaleshwar, a popular hill station of Maharashtra. The fort stands 1080 metres (3543') above sea level and is built on a spur which overlooks the road between the villages of Par and Kinesvar.

An bronze equestrian statue of Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj is present at the fort. It was unveiled by Jawaharlal Nehru, then Prime Minister of India on 30 November 1957. The same year a road was constructed by the Public Works Department from Kumbhrosi village up to fort. A guest house and a small park was built inside the fort in 1960.
