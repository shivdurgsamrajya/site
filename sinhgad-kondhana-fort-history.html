Sinhgad (Kondhana) Fort Maharashtra

  • Travel, Lifestyle, Trekking, Explore
  • 00 Comments
  • Wikipedia

Sinhagad Fort, also known as Sinhgad or Sinhgarh Fort, is a historic fort located near Pune, Maharashtra, India. With a rich history dating back to the 17th century and its stunning natural surroundings, Sinhagad Fort is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Sinhagad Fort is situated approximately 30 kilometers southwest of Pune city. Its geographic coordinates are approximately 18.3653° N latitude and 73.7516° E longitude.

HISTORY OF THE ATTRACTION

Originally known as "Kondhana Fort," Sinhagad Fort earned its current name after a daring night battle led by the Maratha warrior Tanaji Malusare. This historical site witnessed numerous conflicts and played a pivotal role in the Maratha Empire's history.

Sinhagad Fort holds immense historical significance, serving as a testament to the bravery and valor of warriors like Tanaji Malusare. Its strategic importance cannot be overstated, making it a crucial stronghold for various dynasties throughout history.

The fort boasts impressive architectural features, including sturdy gates, massive bastions, and robust walls. Its layout includes distinct sections, each with its unique structures and historical importance.

How to Reach:

By Air

The nearest major airport to Sinhagad Fort is the Pune International Airport (IATA: PNQ). The fort is approximately 45-50 kilometers away from the Pune International Airport.

By Train

Pune Junction is the main railway station serving Pune. Sinhagad Fort is approximately 30 kilometers from Pune Junction.

By Road

Sinhagad Fort is located at a distance of approximately 30 kilometers from Pune city center.

One of the common routes is via Sinhagad Road. You can take this road and follow the signboards to reach the fort. The road offers scenic views along the way.

Pune has a well-developed public transportation system. You can also use local buses or hire a taxi to reach the fort.

Key Attractions:

Tanaji Malusare Samadhi: A memorial dedicated to the heroic Tanaji Malusare.

Kali Temple: A revered temple that attracts devotees and tourists alike.

Sinhagad Fort Museum: Discover artifacts and historical exhibits.

Rajaram Maharaj Samadhi: Pay your respects at the memorial of Chhatrapati Rajaram Maharaj.

Sinhagad Fort is a mecca for trekkers and adventure enthusiasts. Various trekking routes cater to different skill levels, offering an exhilarating journey amid scenic beauty.

Cuisine:

Savor the local cuisine and snacks available at the base of the fort. Delight in dishes like "Pithla Bhakri" and "Bhutta" (corn on the cob) while taking in the panoramic views.

Best Time to Visit:

The best time to visit Sinhagad Fort is during the monsoon season when the surroundings are lush and green. The fort takes on a unique charm during this time, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers.
