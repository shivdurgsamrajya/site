Tikona Fort History Maharashtra

  • 00 Comments

Tikona Fort, also known as Vitandgad, is a historic hill fort located in the Maval region of Maharashtra, India. It is a popular trekking destination and offers a glimpse into the rich history and natural beauty of the area.

Tikona Fort, nestled in the Maval region of Maharashtra, is a captivating hill fort with a storied past and scenic vistas. Trekkers and history enthusiasts alike are drawn to this fort's allure.

Tikona Fort is situated in the Maval region of Maharashtra, approximately 60 kilometers from Pune city. Its geographic coordinates are approximately 18.6619° N latitude and 73.5142° E longitude.

HISTORY OF THE ATTRACTION

Tikona Fort has a historical legacy dating back to the 16th century. It served as a strategic outpost during various dynasties, including the Marathas and the Mughals.

Tikona Fort boasts impressive architectural features, including sturdy walls and gates. Its layout includes various sections, each with unique structures and historical importance.

The fort's strategic location made it a key stronghold for rulers throughout history, and its historical significance is a testament to the region's heritage.

How to Reach:

By Air

The nearest major airport to Tikona Fort is Pune International Airport (IATA: PNQ). Tikona Fort is approximately 75 kilometers from Pune International Airport.

By Train

The nearest railway station to Tikona Fort is Pune Junction. Tikona Fort is approximately 65 kilometers from Pune Junction.

By Road

Tikona Fort is located at a distance of approximately 60 kilometers from Pune city center.

One common route is via the Pune-Mumbai Expressway. You can take this expressway and then exit at Lonavala. From Lonavala, follow the road signs to Tikona Fort. The journey offers scenic views.

Pune has a well-developed public transportation system. You can also use local buses or hire a taxi to reach Tikona Fort.

Key Attractions:

Trimbak Darwaja: The fort's main entrance gate.

Pandava Caves: Discover the ancient Pandava Caves near the fort.

Stunning Views: Enjoy panoramic views of the Sahyadri mountain range and the surrounding landscape.

Cuisine:

Relish local cuisine and snacks available at nearby eateries, such as "Misal Pav" and "Vada Pav."

Best Time to Visit:

The best time to visit Tikona Fort is during the monsoon season when the landscape is lush and the weather is pleasant. The post-monsoon period also offers excellent trekking conditions.
